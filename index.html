What are "hidden reserves" and how can they be built up?
Answer: Hidden reserves are undisclosed profits or assets that a company sets aside on its balance sheet. These reserves are not reported in the company’s financial statements and not disclosed to the public. Their purpose is to strengthen the company’s financial position without disclosing the full extent of profits. They arise from pessimistic evaluation or overevaluation of debts. Hidden reserves occur from depreciation of currency, valuation of provisions, other forms of depreciation etc. They also exist to protect creditors (legislation)

What is Pareto Optimality?
Answer: Pareto Optimality is a term associated with game theory. In a non-zero-sum multi-player game, a pareto optimality between all players is achieved when any move / action that would make one player better off will automatically make the others worse. Base price is 100 -> you pay 70 per unit Triggered volume discount: discount applied to units in certain ranges with given discounts Here average price per piece is 85 instead of 80, because 10% discount is applied to the first 100-199 for a price of 90 EUR and then 200-299 20% with a price of 80 EUR -> average is 85 EURFor the 30% discount we have: 30% for those over 300 so 70 EUR, 20 % for 200-299 and 10% for 100-199. So (90 + 80 + 70) / 3 = 80 EUR average per piece

What is Risk Surcharge Factor?
Answer: Risk Surcharge Factor = P(certain_risk) * P(detect_that_risk) * Severity(risk) e.g: P(occurrence) = 0.2 P(detection) = 0.7 Severity(error) = 0.08 (of the original effort) Risk Surcharge Factor = 0.2 * 0.7 * 0.08 = 0.0112. If Project effort = 1 500 000 EUR -> risk costs are 0.0112 * 1 500 000 = 16 800 EUR

Question:
What is a profit and loss statement and how is it structured?
Answer: A profit and loss statement is a financial report that summarizes the revenues, costs and expenses during a period of time. It is different from a Balance Sheet, that only shows a snapshot of liabilities and assets at a certain time. It can be staggered-down or have two columns for debit and credit. Debit is what is sold, credit is the revenue and earnings. The difference between sum(Credit) – sum(debit) is profit or equity. Profit is written down in debit but its actually first calculated by subtracting credit. If the balance is not equal there is either profit (equity) or loss.
